Smart pressing wins games. This setup teaches players to recognize WHEN to press, not just how. One area, 8 triggers, maximum learning. When players understand pressing triggers, they create more attacking opportunities and defend more effectively.
HEAVY TOUCH
IMMEDIATE PRESSURE

Ball 2+ yards from body = press trigger. Nearest defender closes immediately, others cut passing lanes.

BACK TO GOAL
PREVENT TURN

Can't see behind = vulnerable. Press quickly to prevent turn, teammates cover sideways options.

BAD FIRST TOUCH
POUNCE ON ERROR

Poor control = opportunity. Anticipate difficult passes, position to win loose balls quickly.

SIDEWAYS PASS
TEAM TRIGGER

Lateral pass = no forward option. Whole team shifts to press and force mistakes together.

ISOLATED PLAYER
OUTNUMBER

No support = trap them. Two press while others block escape routes. Force error or bad pass.

RUSHED PASS
READ & INTERCEPT

Pressure = poor passes. Position in lanes, read body language, intercept for quick counter.

LOST BALL
6-SECOND RULE

Immediate counter-press within 6 seconds. Closest presses while others recover shape.

POOR POSITION
EXPLOIT BLINDSIDE

Closed body = limited options. Press from blind side to force mistakes and win possession.

COACHING POINTS
Recognition First Players must SEE triggers before reacting. Use repetition to build automatic responses.
3-Second Window Best pressing happens within 3 seconds of trigger. After that, opposition organizes.
Team Coordination Individual pressing = chasing. Teach collective pressing with clear roles and support.
When NOT to Press Consider position, energy, and game situation. Smart pressing beats constant pressing.
Progressive Training Week 1: Recognize triggers. Week 2: Coordinate pressure. Week 3: Counter-press integration.
Match Transfer Point out triggers during games. Connect training scenarios to real match situations.

Smart pressing creates more attacking opportunities than individual brilliance.
