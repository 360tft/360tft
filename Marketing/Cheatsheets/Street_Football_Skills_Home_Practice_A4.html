Drive past any park today and there won't be a football in sight. Kids don't play in the streets anymore. Those informal games taught valuable football lessons - pulling balls out of the air, dribbling multiple players, finishing under pressure. Here's how to replicate those skills at home with basic equipment.
WORLDY SKILLS
PULLING BALL FROM AIR

Throw ball in air, control with top of foot. Don't bring foot to ball - let ball come to foot. Withdraw on contact to cushion.

CONE DRIBBLING
MULTIPLE DEFENDERS

Use cones, jumpers, shoes - anything! Dribble around them as fast as possible while keeping control. Perfect one or two moves.

FINISHING ON MOVE
AFTER DRIBBLE

After dribbling around obstacles, still need quality finish. Use mini-goals or targets. Finish must be accurate when tired.

WALL PASSING
WALLY GAME

Kick ball against wall, control return. Miss the wall = letter. Develops speed, balance, touch, and accuracy under pressure.

FOOT SPEED
TOE TAPS

Rapid toe taps on ball. How many before getting tired? Builds foot speed needed to reach ball quickly in Wally game.

WALL ACCURACY
EDGE TARGETING

Aim for edge of wall to make next shot difficult. Football skittles - knock ball off cone from 20-30 yards for accuracy practice.

CROSSING PRACTICE
HEADERS & VOLLEYS

Cross to targets or into goal from wide positions. Crosses need to be in air and accurate. Vary depth and pace by moving target.

JUGGLING BALANCE
KEEP-UP CHALLENGE

Excellent way to improve balance and touch. Before wall returns, quickly look over both shoulders to "check space" like in matches.

MAKING STREET SKILLS TRANSFER TO MATCHES
Train at 100% Intensity Not only are you learning, but tiring yourself while doing so. Helps fitness and ensures energy in later stages of matches.
Develop Awareness When dribbling multiple objects, be aware of where space is and where next "defender" is positioned. Look before you receive.
Master One or Two Moves Don't learn lots of moves - perfect one or two and apply them repeatedly. Quality over quantity in skill development.
Practice Under Fatigue Street games were tiring. You needed quality finish after exhausting dribble. Train when tired for match realism.
Use Basic Equipment Cones, jumpers, shoes, wall, spare ball. Sometimes you just need a ball! Don't overcomplicate the setup.
Build Defending Skills Practice standing tackles, not slide tackles. Need to win ball standing up so you can attack immediately after winning it.

Children are spending less time outdoors. These factors can be overcome using the tips above.
