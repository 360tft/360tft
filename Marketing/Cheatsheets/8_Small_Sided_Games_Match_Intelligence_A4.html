The format looks right, but the outcomes don't land. Players run around, there's no coaching moment, no game transfer. Each SSG below creates pressure, forces decisions, and replicates the things players actually face in matches. Because they create learning, not just activity.
1v1 TO GOAL
DRIBBLING & FINISHING

One attacks, one defends. Two goals at either end. Simple format, high repetition. Sharpens finishing in tight spaces and builds confidence attacking defenders directly.

2v2+2 NEUTRAL
PLAYING OFF MOVEMENT

Two teams of two with neutral players at each end. Focus on support play, angle of movement, and combination using bounce passes and third-man runs.

3v3+1 JOKER
COMPACTNESS & PRESSING

Joker plays with possession team. Extra player forces defending side to press together or collapse space. Coaches shifting and timing of pressure.

4v4+2 WIDE
SWITCHING PLAY

Neutral players on sidelines act as bounce players. Goal is to break pressure by playing through and around. Brilliant for coaching width and switching.

5v5+2 CENTRAL
CENTRAL OVERLOAD

One joker in middle acts as pivot. Teams must play off this central point to link play. Teaches recognising when to play into central zones.

6v6 TIGHT SPACE
PRESSURE DECISIONS

No jokers, just tight pitch. Ideal for developing scanning, first touch under pressure, and support movement. Players learn to stay calm when space is restricted.

7v7+1 FALSE 9
CENTRAL SUPPORT

Central joker creates midfield-attack link. Think false 9 or advanced pivot. Works best for developing ideas around linking midfield to attack.

8v8+2 TRANSFER
COORDINATED PRESSING

Ball starts one side, can only transfer across through jokers. Defending team must shift, screen, anticipate. Brilliant for rest defence and compactness.

HOW TO COACH THESE EFFECTIVELY
Short Interventions Freeze, ask questions, reset. "Where's the space?" "Who's the extra player?" Keep players thinking, not explaining.
Coach the Key Moments Don't just let them play. The real value is in what you say while it's happening. Highlight pressing triggers, support movements.
Game Connection "Remember when this happened in Saturday's match?" Connect SSG moments to real game situations they've experienced.
Maximum Outcomes, Minimal Setup These formats don't require loads of setup. You get maximum learning with minimal time wasted on organisation.
Layer Into Sessions Use as core of whole practice or layer rondos before, match play after. They work as beginning intensity or finishing competition.
Condition for Purpose Add constraints that emphasise specific learning. Touch limits, time pressure, or space restrictions force the outcomes you want.

The SSGs in this cheatsheet aren't magic. They're not flashy. But they work.
