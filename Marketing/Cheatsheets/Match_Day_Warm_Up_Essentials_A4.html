Great warm-ups prepare players physically and mentally. This systematic approach activates muscles, improves mobility, sharpens technical skills, and builds match readiness. 20 minutes that can determine the first 20 minutes of your game.
20-MINUTE WARM-UP STRUCTURE
5 MIN
ACTIVATION
Light movement, muscle activation, heart rate increase
5 MIN
MOBILITY
Dynamic stretching, joint preparation, movement quality
7 MIN
TECHNICAL
Ball work, passing, first touch, match actions
3 MIN
MATCH PREP
High intensity, game situations, mental preparation
LIGHT JOGGING
ACTIVATION

Easy-paced running to increase core temperature. Include changes of direction and backward jogging.

DYNAMIC STRETCHING
MOBILITY

Leg swings, arm circles, hip circles, walking lunges. Prepare joints for full range of motion.

MOVEMENT PATTERNS
COORDINATION

High knees, butt kicks, side shuffles, carioca. Build coordination and movement quality.

BALL MASTERY
TECHNICAL

Individual touches, juggling, foundation moves. Get comfortable feeling of ball on foot.

PASSING PAIRS
TECHNICAL

Short passes, increasing distance and pace. Include first-time passes and different surfaces.

SHOOTING PRACTICE
FINISHING

Low-intensity shooting to get feel for goal. Build confidence and sharpen finishing touch.

SMALL SIDED GAMES
MATCH PREP

3v3 or 4v4 games at moderate intensity. Include match actions like pressing and quick passing.

HIGH INTENSITY SPRINTS
MATCH READY

Short, sharp sprints to activate fast-twitch muscles. Prepare body for match intensity.

COACHING POINTS
Progressive Intensity Start at 40% intensity, build to 85% by end. Never go 100% in warm-up - save peak for match.
Individual Needs Some players need more activation, others more technical work. Allow slight variations.
Weather Adaptation Cold conditions: longer activation phase. Hot conditions: more hydration breaks, shade when possible.
Mental Preparation Use warm-up to build confidence. Positive touches, successful actions, encouraging words.
Injury Prevention Never skip mobility phase. Address any stiffness or discomfort before it becomes a problem.
Team Bonding Use as opportunity to connect team. Pair exercises, group activities, shared focus.

A great warm-up sets the foundation for great performance. Prepare well, play well.
