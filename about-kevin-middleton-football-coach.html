The Early Days

My coaching journey began like many others - with passion, but little direction. Fresh-faced and eager, I thought coaching was about knowing more drills than the next person. I was wrong.

My first season coaching an U12 team was humbling. Despite having endless energy and what I thought were great sessions, the players weren't improving. Parents were polite but concerned. I was failing the kids who trusted me to help them get better.

That's when I realized coaching isn't about what you know - it's about how effectively you can transfer that knowledge into improved player performance.

I spent that summer studying everything I could find about player development, sports psychology, and motor learning. But more importantly, I started watching my players more carefully - not just what they were doing wrong, but why they were doing it wrong.

The Birth of 360TFT

The "360" represents the complete player - technical, tactical, physical, and mental development all working together. "TFT" stands for "Think Fast, Think Football" - the ability to make good decisions under pressure.

But 360TFT became more than a methodology. It became a community of coaches who believe that every player deserves systematic, purposeful development. Not just the talented ones. Not just the ones whose parents can afford private coaching. Every player.

The moment I realized 360TFT was working was when a parent coach told me: "My son doesn't just play better now - he thinks about the game differently." That's when I knew we were onto something special.

Word spread through coaching networks. Other coaches started asking for my session plans, my development frameworks, my "secrets." But I realized that giving someone a drill isn't the same as teaching them how to coach. That's when I decided to build something bigger.

My Coaching Philosophy

After 15+ years of coaching, certain principles have become non-negotiable for me. These aren't just theories - they're battle-tested truths that separate coaches who develop players from coaches who just run training sessions.

Players don't remember what you taught them - they remember how you made them feel while they were learning it. This changed everything about how I approach coaching interactions.

Technique without decision-making is just juggling. Technical ability means nothing if players can't apply it under pressure, in the right moment, with teammates and opponents around them.

Every session must build on the last one. Random training sessions create random players. Systematic development creates consistent improvement.

The best coaches make themselves obsolete. My job isn't to create players who depend on me for answers - it's to create players who can solve problems independently.

Today & What's Next

Today, 360TFT has grown beyond what I ever imagined. We have coaches using our methodologies in professional academies, grassroots clubs, and school programs across six continents. The community includes parent coaches taking their first steps and experienced professionals refining their craft.

But I'm not done. The Football Coaching Academy represents the next evolution - a place where serious coaches can access cutting-edge research, learn from each other, and stay ahead of the curve in player development.

My mission remains the same: to help coaches develop better players, faster. Because when coaches improve, players improve. And when players improve, the beautiful game gets a little more beautiful.

Every coach who joins our community, every player who develops through our methods, every session that creates genuine improvement - that's what drives me. That's what 360TFT is really about.

Core Coaching Principles

The fundamental beliefs that guide everything I do

🎯

Purpose-Driven Development

Every exercise, every session, every interaction must have a clear developmental purpose. Players can sense when training lacks direction, and they respond accordingly.

🧠

Decision-Making First

Technical skills are tools, but decision-making is what makes players effective. I teach players not just how to pass, but when to pass, where to pass, and why.

⚡

Pressure Creates Diamonds

Training without pressure creates training ground players. Real development happens when players must execute skills while under physical, mental, and time pressure.

🔄

Systematic Progression

Random sessions create random players. Every session must build logically on the previous one, creating layers of understanding and ability.

💝

Love the Process

Players who love training become players who love improving. My job is to create environments where growth feels like play, not work.

🚀

Coach Yourself Out of a Job

The mark of great coaching isn't players who depend on you, but players who can think and adapt independently. I create problem-solvers, not followers.

Beyond Football

While football is my professional passion, I believe the best coaches are well-rounded people who bring diverse experiences to their coaching.

I'm fascinated by psychology, sports science, and how people learn. I read extensively about motor learning, cognitive development, and performance psychology because understanding people makes me a better coach.

  • Every player is fighting a battle you know nothing about - lead with empathy
  • The best learning happens just outside the comfort zone - not too easy, not impossible
  • Mistakes are data, not failures - treat them as learning opportunities
  • Culture beats strategy every time - build the right environment first
  • Your energy as a coach is contagious - make sure it's worth catching
